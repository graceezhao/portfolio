Designing a Merchant Assortment Tool

Product Management
Product Design
Retail

Description

I worked on a product that helped a national retailer optimize in-store assortments using customer data and regional constraints. The tool allowed merchants to compare strategies and maximize revenue and shelf efficiency.

Overview

I joined the project initially to support data analysis, but as the team moved from backend development into productization, I stepped in to help define product requirements and shape the front-end experience. I worked closely with engineers, acting as a bridge between raw data workflows and a usable merchant-facing tool.


Initial Problem

Early on, the product existed primarily as a backend system supported by manual Excel analysis. To test and validate the logic, I used the backend directly to manually re-assort a category: an error-prone and time-intensive process. While effective (the new assortment increased projected category revenue by over $5M), it highlighted a clear need for a front-end experience that surfaced the right data, handled edge cases, and made decision-making scalable for merchants.



The manual and confusing processes when working in the background shaped my design priorities: transparency, error visibility, and flexibility for merchant control.

Final Prototype

After 2+ weeks of creating product specs, iterating, and working with our clients + the dev team, the final experience focused on making complex assortment logic understandable and actionable across the following features within Category Catalogs:

Grouped View: Merchants could quickly see which items were assigned, unassigned, or missing required attributes.


Attribute & Competitor Comparison View: An attribute table surfaced key product dimensions, e.g., own brand vs. national brand, enabling easy comparison across competitors.


Item Scoring Visualization: Items were scored across the category, with adjustable weights to optimize for different goals like space productivity or base price.


Final Thoughts

Shipping this product was especially meaningful because I had experienced the problem firsthand, manually doing the work the tool was meant to replace. Throughout the project, I worked on data analysis, backend debugging, product management, and design, often simultaneously, which was fun and allowed me to have a lot of autonomy.

I was able to be scrappy and translate complexity into usable products; this was a major moment of growth for me as both a consultant and a designer, and I'm grateful for the team of BCG Data Scientists, Engineers, and Consultants, as well as the client's Merchant & Planning teams for the experience!
