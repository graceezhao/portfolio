Overview

I worked on building a patient portal/app to enhance patient engagement and access to services.

Our key question was: How can we build a seamless hospital app that empowers patients through personalized messaging and comprehensive data compilation?



Vaccinations Exploration & Initial Stages

In the early prototyping phase of the Vaccinations module, I explored how vaccination data is structured: what's required, how completion and dates are tracked, and how to clearly surface status to users. With COVID-19 still recent, I also considered no-contact ways to verify vaccination, especially for COVID shots. This was a highly exploratory phase, focused on understanding data flow and usability to test core display and interaction ideas.

Exploring color-coded labels, vaccination tracking timelines, vaccine brand name, and multiple doses

After four rounds of user testing, we uncovered key insights that shaped the direction of the Vaccinations module:

1) Parents especially valued the ability to send their child's immunization records.
2) Users had different expectations for COVID vaccination records compared to routine vaccines.
3) Participants found the ability emailing records to third parties directly from the app useful.

Final Vaccinations Prototype


Takeaways & Improvements:

The final Vaccinations module presented immunization records in a clear, scannable view that shows vaccination status, dose completion, and fast sharing. High-priority use cases were surfaced through Download and Share flows.

This design reduced ambiguity around vaccination records, supported frequent sharing for dependents, and aligned with different expectations for COVID vs. routine vaccines, transforming vaccination data into an intuitive, actionable experience.


Visit Summaries Exploration & Initial Stages

In early iterations, my goal was to prioritize clarity and actionability, surfacing the most important takeaways first while reducing cognitive load and exploring how visit information could better connect to related workflows like billing, insurance, and messaging.

We ran two rounds of testing, and found the following key takeaways:

1) Users prioritized visit outcomes and next steps over care team details.
2) For caregivers, visit summaries were critical for coordinating care and sharing information.
3) Patients struggled to connect visit information with billing, insurance, and health records.

Next steps included to continue iterative testing as additional visit summary capabilities and cross-module integrations (e.g., billing, messaging).




Final Thoughts

Thank you to the support I got from everyone on the team. Special shoutout to the designers who welcomed me both in person and virtually.
